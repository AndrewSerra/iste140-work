Real Life Examples

Quora/Stack Overflow/Reddit: Upvotes for Points

The pattern that has dominated the technology industry has been upvotes or likes on specific content. The more positive reinforcement is done toward the content, the more popularity and view it will receive.

This can be considered extrinsic motivation as you can gain status through rewards and punishments. A more technical name for the technique used on these platforms is called intermittent reinforcement. This is done by providing rewards at irregular intervals. Giving badges, upvotes, points, downvotes, dislikes, status names, and more are all similar applications of the same concept.

Free Rice: Random act of kindness

Free Rice is a game where one can practice English vocabulary by selecting the word in the options that have the same meaning as the given word. In return, the user receivesâ€¦ Nothing. This is not exactly correct, anyone who plays will see a bowl of rice getting more and more full as correct answers are selected. Then for each answer, the user will be automatically donating a certain amount of rice to people who need it.

There is no external motivator except for social help, and the satisfaction of being a part of something bigger. This is the core aim of any game. You become a collaborator to the community who is playing the same game, practice your skills and donate rice. This meaningful act is the intrinsic motivator in this game.
